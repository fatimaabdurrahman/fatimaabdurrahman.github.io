Fatima Abdurrahman, PhD

Astrophysicist • Educator • Scholar Activist

About Fatima

Fatima Abdurrahman is an academic of many passions: after earning undergraduate degrees in Physics, Astronomy, and Arabic Studies from the University of Maryland, she went on to UC Berkeley's Astronomy Department where she earned her master's and doctoral degrees in Astrophysics. The breadth of her scientific interest and expertise are illustrated in her doctoral thesis, which includes work in the development of adaptive optics instruments, the detection of black holes through gravitational microlensing, and socio-cultural analyses of the spaces of academic physics and astronomy.

Beyond scientific research, Fatima is dedicated to making astronomy and physics education more equitable and accessible by any means necessary. Her work towards this goal has included pedagogical research on the social dynamics of learning spaces, the design of inclusive course curricula, and the development of antiracist educational materials for educators. Besides her work in academic settings, she brings her love for astronomy to the broader community through public talks on her scientific research and scholar activism.

Scientific Research

During my PhD, the main focal points of my scientific research were adaptive optics instrumentation and black hole microlensing. You can learn more about these works below, and see my full publication list on NASA/ADS. You can also learn about my education reserach below.

Detecting Black Holes with Gravitational Lensing

I search for hard-to-find astronomical objects—primarily isolated, stellar mass black holes—using the fact that objects with mass will bend the direction light travels, creating distortions in our images of the night sky.

High Resolution Imaging with Adaptive Optics

I worked on a team building an adaptive optics instrument, which allows telescopes to take images of astronomical objects without distortion from the Earth's atmosphere.
